## The Problem We See

### The Two Clocks Problem

Every enterprise has built infrastructure for **what is true now** (the State Clock):
- CRM knows the deal value
- TMS knows the shipment status
- WMS knows the inventory level

Almost nothing exists for **why it became true** (the Event Clock):
- Why was this carrier selected?
- Who approved this exception?
- What precedent justified this discount?
- Why did we override the system recommendation?

> "We've built trillion-dollar infrastructure for what's true now. Almost nothing for why it became true." — Animesh Koratana

### The Logistics Reality

In Indian logistics, decisions happen across:
- **Slack threads** that get buried
- **WhatsApp groups** that nobody can search
- **Phone calls** that aren't recorded
- **People's heads** who might leave tomorrow

When your best logistics coordinator quits, their institutional knowledge walks out the door.

## The Technical POV: Context Graphs & Decision Traces

### What Decision Traces Capture

At decision time, we capture:
- **What inputs** were gathered (from 6+ systems)
- **What policy** was evaluated
- **What exception** was invoked
- **Who approved**
- **What precedent** was referenced
- **What outcome** resulted

This complete trace **only exists in the execution path**. Warehouses see it after the fact. CRMs never see it.

### Why This Matters

| Without Decision Traces | With Decision Traces |
|------------------------|---------------------|
| "Carrier: Delhivery" | "Delhivery selected because: pincode performance 94%, cost optimal, customer preference. Alternative BlueDart rejected: 12% higher cost. No exception." |
| "NDR: Resolved" | "Resolved via reattempt. Similar case NDR-089 succeeded. Customer confirmed availability via WhatsApp. Agent confidence 87%." |
| "Rate: ₹3,500/MT" | "Rate approved at ₹3,500/MT. Market benchmark ₹3,800. 8% below market due to volume commitment. VP approved deviation from standard 5% threshold." |

### The Compounding Effect

> "The agents aren't building the context graph—they're solving problems worth paying for. The context graph is the exhaust."

Better context → More capable agents → More deployments → More trajectories → Better context

## Industry-Specific POV Applications

### Steel & Metals
**Context**: Plant logistics, heavy loads, long-term contracts
**POV application**: Decision traces for carrier selection under plant TAT pressure. Precedent-based exception handling for detention. Ecosystem coordination across multiple plants.

### Chemical & Oil/Gas

**POV application**: Full audit trail for regulatory compliance. Decision traces capture WHY a carrier was approved for hazmat. Context graphs enable faster compliance without manual reviews.

## The Uncomfortable Truth

### Puneet's View on TMS Commoditization

> "If something generic could cut it, that would be taken by the horizontal AI labs. What would work out well is software on the orchestration layer."

| Category | AI Impact |
|----------|-----------|
| **Deterministic execution (TMS/WMS)** | Gets commoditized |
| **Judgment-intensive coordination (S&OP)** | Expands dramatically |
| **Orchestration layer (decision capture)** | Wins |

**Implication**: Pure TMS is at risk. The winning position is **decision orchestration that captures context**.
