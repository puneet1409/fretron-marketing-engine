# Event Playbooks

Detailed execution playbooks for each event type.

## Conference/Trade Show Playbook

### 6 Weeks Before

**Account Selection**
- Pull attendee list (if available)
- Cross-reference with target account list
- Identify priority contacts (5-10 per day of event)
- Research each contact (LinkedIn, company news)

**Logistics**
- Confirm booth location and setup requirements
- Order collateral and giveaways
- Book team travel and accommodation
- Schedule team prep meeting

**Meeting Goal Setting**
```
Event days: [X]
Meetings per day: 7-10
Total meeting target: [X × 8]
Conversion target (to opportunity): 20%
```

### 4 Weeks Before: Outreach Begins

**Email Template (First Touch)**
```
Subject: Meeting at [Event Name]?

Hi [Name],

I see you're attending [Event Name] next month - I'll be there
representing Fretron.

I've been following [Company]'s growth in [industry] and thought
it might be valuable to connect. We help companies like [Similar
Company] with [specific challenge].

Would you have 20 minutes to meet at the event? I'm flexible on
timing - [specific day] works well for me.

Best,
[Signature]
```

**LinkedIn Sequence**
- Week 4: Connect (mention event in request)
- Week 3: Like/comment on their content
- Week 2: DM with meeting request
- Week 1: Final confirmation

### 2 Weeks Before: Confirmation

**Follow-up Email**
```
Subject: Re: Meeting at [Event Name]

Hi [Name],

Just confirming our meeting at [Event Name]:
- Date: [Day]
- Time: [Time]
- Location: [Booth #X / Café / etc.]

Looking forward to discussing [specific topic].

Best,
[Signature]
```

**For Non-Responders**
```
Subject: Quick hello at [Event]?

Hi [Name],

I know schedules get packed at events - if a formal meeting
doesn't work, I'd love to at least say hello.

I'll be at booth [X] if you're passing by.

Best,
[Signature]
```

### 1 Week Before: Prep

**Checklist**
- [ ] Research each confirmed meeting (10 min each)
- [ ] Prepare 2-3 specific talking points per meeting
- [ ] Print calendar with all meetings
- [ ] Prepare lead capture method (app, cards, notes)
- [ ] Brief booth staff on key accounts
- [ ] Prepare collateral to share
- [ ] Charge devices, download offline materials

### On-Site: Daily Rhythm

**Morning (30 min before floor opens)**
- Review day's scheduled meetings
- Brief team on priority accounts
- Check booth setup

**During Event**
- Rotate booth coverage
- Execute scheduled meetings
- Opportunistic networking
- Real-time note capture

**Evening (15 min)**
- Quick debrief with team
- Prioritize next-day follow-ups
- Update CRM with notes

### Meeting Execution

**15-Minute Meeting Structure**
- 0-2 min: Rapport/context
- 2-5 min: Their situation/challenges
- 5-10 min: Relevant Fretron value
- 10-13 min: Proof point/example
- 13-15 min: Next step agreement

**Meeting Notes Template**
```
## Meeting: [Name] - [Company]
**Date**: [Date/Time]
**Attendees**: [Names]

### Key Points
- Current state: [How they manage logistics today]
- Challenges: [Top 1-2 pain points]
- Interest: [What resonated]
- Objections: [Concerns raised]

### Next Steps
- [ ] [Action item 1]
- [ ] [Action item 2]

### Follow-up
- Send: [Specific content/info]
- Schedule: [Next meeting type]
- Timeline: [When]
```

## Executive Dinner Playbook

### Guest Selection Criteria

**Ideal Attendee Profile**
- Director+ level
- From target accounts or existing customers
- Mix of industries for cross-pollination
- 10-15 total (intimate conversation)

**Invitation Ratio**
- Invite 25-30 for 12-15 attendees
- Expect 40-50% acceptance rate
- Over-invite early, manage to target

### Event Format

**Suggested Agenda (3 hours)**
```
6:00 PM - Arrivals, networking (drinks)
6:30 PM - Welcome, introductions (5 min each)
7:00 PM - Dinner, facilitated discussion
8:30 PM - Key insights summary
9:00 PM - Informal networking, close
```

### Facilitation Guide

**Opening**
- Welcome and thank attendees
- Brief Fretron context (2 min max)
- Set ground rules (Chatham House, no selling)
- Round-table introductions

**Discussion Prompts**
1. "What's the biggest logistics challenge you're facing this year?"
2. "How is your approach to [topic] evolving?"
3. "What's working that others should know about?"
4. "What do you wish you'd done differently?"

**Closing**
- Summarize key themes heard
- Thank attendees
- Mention follow-up (no hard sell)
- Open networking

### Venue Selection

**Requirements**
- Private dining room
- Round or square table (no long tables)
- Quality F&B (reflects on brand)
- Easy parking/access
- Quiet enough for conversation

**Budget Guidance**
- ₹3,000-5,000 per person for quality venue
- 15 attendees = ₹45,000-75,000 F&B
- Speaker/facilitator fee if external
- Gifts optional but appreciated

### Follow-up

**Day After**
```
Subject: Thank you for last evening

Hi [Name],

Thank you for joining us last night. I really appreciated
your perspective on [specific point they raised].

As promised, here's [resource discussed]:
[Link]

Would love to continue the conversation. [Specific next step
if appropriate, or leave open]

Best,
[Signature]
```

## Webinar Playbook

### Planning (4 Weeks Before)

**Format Options**
| Format | Best For | Engagement Level |
|--------|----------|------------------|
| Solo presentation | Thought leadership | Medium |
| Panel (3-4 speakers) | Multiple perspectives | Medium |
| Interview | Expert credibility | High |
| Demo + Q&A | Product interest | High |

**Recommended: Interview Format**
- Borrows expert's credibility
- Guest promotes to their network
- More engaging than solo
- Easier to prepare

**Speaker Selection Criteria**
- Recognized in industry
- Active social presence (will promote)
- Good on camera
- Available for prep and promotion

### Production (2 Weeks Before)

**Technical Setup**
- Platform: Zoom/StreamYard/similar
- Backup: Phone dial-in for speakers
- Recording: Always record
- Slides: Minimal, supporting only

**Promotion Plan**
```
4 weeks: Announcement email + social
3 weeks: Speaker highlight posts
2 weeks: Reminder email
1 week: Final push + LinkedIn event
1 day: Reminder email
1 hour: Final reminder
```

**Registration Page Elements**
- Clear title with benefit
- Date/time with timezone
- Speaker bio and photo
- 3 bullet points: what they'll learn
- Simple form (name, email, company)

### Execution (Day Of)

**Pre-Webinar (30 min before)**
- Tech check with speakers
- Review flow and transitions
- Test screen sharing
- Prepare backup slides

**During Webinar**
- Welcome and housekeeping (2 min)
- Speaker intros (3 min)
- Content (40-45 min)
- Q&A (10-15 min)
- Close with CTA (2 min)

**Engagement Tactics**
- Poll in first 5 minutes
- Q&A throughout (read periodically)
- Call out attendee companies
- Interactive elements every 10 min

### Follow-up (Post-Webinar)

**Immediate (Same Day)**
- Send recording to all registrants
- Post highlight clip to social
- Share with sales team

**Day 1-3**
- Attendee follow-up (engaged)
- No-show follow-up (recording)
- Speaker thank you

**Week 1-2**
- Repurpose: Blog post from content
- Repurpose: Social clips (3-5)
- Repurpose: Email newsletter feature
- Sales follow-up on hot leads

## Customer Event Playbook

### Purpose

Customer events serve multiple goals:
- Strengthen relationships
- Drive expansion conversations
- Generate references and referrals
- Build community
- Gather product feedback

### Format Options

**User Group Meeting (Quarterly)**
- 2-3 hours
- 10-20 customers
- Product updates + feedback
- Peer networking

**Customer Conference (Annual)**
- Full day or multi-day
- 50-200 customers
- Keynotes, breakouts, networking
- Product roadmap, training

**Executive Roundtable**
- Half day
- 8-12 senior leaders
- Strategic discussion
- No selling, relationship focus

### User Group Template

**Agenda (3 hours)**
```
9:00 - Welcome, introductions
9:15 - Product update (30 min)
9:45 - Customer spotlight (15 min)
10:00 - Break
10:15 - Roadmap preview + feedback (45 min)
11:00 - Open discussion / networking
11:45 - Wrap-up, next steps
12:00 - Close (optional lunch)
```

**Customer Spotlight**
- Invite 1-2 customers to present
- 10-15 min each
- Their journey, results, lessons
- Q&A from peers

### Success Metrics

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Attendance rate | 70%+ of invited |
| NPS score | 50+ |
| Expansion conversations | 30%+ of attendees |
| Reference commitments | 10%+ of attendees |
| Follow-up meetings booked | 50%+ of attendees |
