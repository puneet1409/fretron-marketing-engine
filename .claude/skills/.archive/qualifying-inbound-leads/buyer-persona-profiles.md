# Buyer Persona Deep Profiles

Comprehensive profiles for Fretron's key buying committee members.

# PERSONA 1: THE LOGISTICS HEAD (CHAMPION)

## Profile Overview

**Titles**: VP Logistics, Head of Logistics, GM Logistics, SCM Head, VP Supply Chain, Director Logistics

**Reports To**: COO, CEO, or CFO

**Direct Reports**: Dispatch managers, transport managers, fleet managers, warehouse teams

**Typical Background**:
- 15-20 years in logistics/operations
- Worked up from plant operations
- May have MBA or engineering degree
- Experience across multiple companies in industry

---

## Day in the Life

**Morning (8-10 AM)**:
- Reviews overnight dispatch status
- Checks for delivery exceptions
- Handles escalations from previous day
- Prioritizes fires to fight

**Mid-Morning (10 AM - 1 PM)**:
- Coordination calls with plant heads
- Carrier/transporter meetings
- Customer complaint handling
- Invoice/payment approvals

**Afternoon (2-5 PM)**:
- Planning meetings
- Cost review discussions
- Team management
- Report preparation

**Evening (5-7 PM)**:
- End-of-day status review
- Next-day planning
- Escalation resolution
- Email catch-up

**Key Frustrations**:
- "I spend 80% of my time firefighting"
- "My team makes 500+ tracking calls daily"
- "I can't get the data I need for decisions"
- "Excel breaks every time we scale"
- "CFO keeps asking questions I can't answer"

---

## Motivations & Goals

### Professional Goals

| Goal | Priority | Fretron Connection |
|------|----------|-------------------|
| Reduce logistics costs | High | Cost optimization, visibility |
| Improve service levels | High | OTIF tracking, proactive alerts |
| Build capable team | Medium | Free team from manual work |
| Gain strategic seat | Medium | Data-driven insights |
| Implement technology | Medium | Modern TMS platform |

### Personal Goals

- Be seen as strategic, not just operational
- Have time for strategic thinking
- Build reputation as innovator
- Reduce stress and firefighting
- Career advancement

### Success Metrics They Care About

1. Freight cost as % of revenue
2. OTIF (On-Time In-Full) %
3. Cost per shipment / per MT-km
4. Customer complaints
5. Team productivity

---

## Information Consumption

### Content Preferences

| Format | Interest Level | Topics |
|--------|---------------|--------|
| Case studies | High | Peer company results |
| Benchmarks | High | Industry comparisons |
| How-to guides | Medium | Tactical improvements |
| Webinars | Medium | If relevant and short |
| White papers | Low | Too theoretical |
| Video | Medium | Short, practical |

### Trusted Sources

- Industry peer conversations
- Industry events/conferences
- LinkedIn (follows industry leaders)
- Industry publications
- Vendor case studies (validated)

### Research Behavior

- Asks peers first
- Google searches for solutions
- Reviews case studies carefully
- Skeptical of vendor claims
- Values references highly

---

## Buying Behavior

### Decision Role

**Primary**: Champion / Technical Buyer
- Drives the evaluation
- Owns the requirements
- Influences heavily
- May not have final budget authority

### Buying Triggers

**Immediate Action**:
- Major delivery failure / customer loss
- New capacity coming online
- CFO cost reduction mandate
- Competitive threat visible

**Near-Term Consideration**:
- Persistent pain reaching threshold
- Team burnout/turnover
- Strategic initiative support
- Peer success story heard

### Objections & Concerns

| Objection | Real Concern | How to Address |
|-----------|--------------|----------------|
| "We've tried systems before" | Fear of failure, wasted effort | Pilot approach, quick time-to-value |
| "My team is already stretched" | Implementation burden | Lightweight implementation, we do the work |
| "How do I know it'll work here?" | Risk of our situation being different | Industry-specific references, pilot |
| "Carriers won't adopt" | Carrier compliance challenge | Show adoption rates, onboarding support |
| "What about our processes?" | Forced process change | We adapt to you |

### Evaluation Criteria

1. **Will it actually solve my pain?** (Visibility, tracking reduction)
2. **Can we implement without disruption?** (Quick, low-resource)
3. **What do similar companies say?** (References critical)
4. **Will my team adopt it?** (Usability, training)
5. **Can I justify the investment?** (ROI clarity)

---

## Engagement Strategy

### Content Journey

**Awareness**: Industry benchmarks, pain point content
**Interest**: How-to guides, case studies
**Consideration**: Free audit, detailed demo
**Decision**: References, pilot proposal, ROI model

### Messaging That Resonates

**Do Say**:
- "Stop tracking. Start optimizing."
- "Free your team from tracking calls"
- "See results in 90 days"
- "We've done this for companies like yours"
- "Low-risk pilot approach"

**Don't Say**:
- "Transform your logistics" (too vague)
- "AI-powered platform" (don't care about tech)
- "Enterprise-grade solution" (sounds expensive)
- "Change management required" (red flag)

### Ideal Touchpoint Sequence

```
Touch 1: Industry insight (relevant pain point)
Touch 2: Case study from their industry
Touch 3: Free audit/assessment offer
Touch 4: Discovery call
Touch 5: Demo with their scenarios
Touch 6: Reference call with peer
Touch 7: Pilot proposal
```

# PERSONA 2: THE CFO (ECONOMIC BUYER)

## Profile Overview

**Titles**: CFO, VP Finance, Finance Director, Head of Finance

**Reports To**: CEO, Board

**Typical Background**:
- CA or MBA Finance
- 15-25 years in finance
- May have consulting background
- Experience across industries

---

## Day in the Life

**Focus Areas**:
- Financial planning and analysis
- Cost management
- Working capital optimization
- Investor/board reporting
- Risk management
- Strategic initiatives

**Key Frustrations**:
- "I don't have visibility into true logistics costs"
- "Freight invoices are always disputed"
- "Every department wants budget for technology"
- "I need ROI proof, not promises"
- "Previous tech investments haven't delivered"

---

## Motivations & Goals

### Professional Goals

| Goal | Priority | Fretron Connection |
|------|----------|-------------------|
| Reduce costs | Very High | Freight cost reduction |
| Improve cash flow | High | Faster reconciliation |
| Risk mitigation | High | Visibility, control |
| Support growth | Medium | Scalable operations |
| Digital transformation | Medium | Modern platform |

### Success Metrics

1. Freight cost as % of revenue
2. Working capital (DSO impact)
3. Cost per unit shipped
4. Budget variance
5. ROI on technology investments

---

## Buying Behavior

### Decision Role

**Primary**: Economic Buyer
- Approves budget
- Signs contract
- Needs business case
- May not be involved until late stage

### What They Need to Approve

1. **Clear ROI**: Specific, believable savings
2. **Risk mitigation**: What if it doesn't work?
3. **Reasonable investment**: Proportional to benefit
4. **Validated claims**: References, not just projections
5. **Implementation feasibility**: Won't disrupt operations

### Objections & Concerns

| Objection | Real Concern | How to Address |
|-----------|--------------|----------------|
| "What's the ROI?" | Need to justify investment | Detailed ROI model with conservative assumptions |
| "We have bigger priorities" | Not convinced of value | Quantify cost of inaction |
| "Previous tech failed to deliver" | Risk aversion | Pilot approach, success-based milestones |
| "Can we do this cheaper?" | Cost sensitivity | TCO comparison, hidden cost of alternatives |
| "What if it doesn't work?" | Risk of wasted investment | Pilot, phased approach, references |

---

## Engagement Strategy

### Content That Resonates

| Content Type | CFO Interest | Notes |
|--------------|-------------|-------|
| ROI calculator | Very High | Must be credible |
| Benchmark data | High | Industry comparisons |
| Case study (financial focus) | High | Specific numbers |
| Business case template | High | Helps internal process |
| TCO analysis | Medium | For comparison |
| Technical details | Low | Delegate to team |

### Messaging That Resonates

**Do Say**:
- "15-25% freight cost reduction with 6-month payback"
- "₹1-2 Cr annual savings for companies your size"
- "Prove it in 90 days before full commitment"
- "Transaction-based pricing—pay for results"
- "Reference CFOs available to speak with"

**Don't Say**:
- "Cutting-edge technology"
- "Digital transformation"
- "Strategic platform"
- Anything without numbers

### Engagement Timing

- Engage after champion is convinced
- Provide business case support
- Offer CFO-to-CFO reference
- Be ready for rapid evaluation once engaged

# PERSONA 3: THE CIO/IT HEAD (TECHNICAL BUYER)

## Profile Overview

**Titles**: CIO, VP IT, IT Head, Head of Digital, Director IT

**Reports To**: CEO, CFO, or COO

**Typical Background**:
- Engineering or CS degree
- 15-20 years in IT
- Mix of development and infrastructure
- May have led digital transformation initiatives

---

## Motivations & Goals

### Professional Goals

| Goal | Priority | Fretron Connection |
|------|----------|-------------------|
| Enable business | High | Solves logistics pain |
| Minimize risk | High | Security, reliability |
| Reduce maintenance | High | Cloud, vendor-managed |
| Integration excellence | Medium | ERP connectivity |
| Innovation | Medium | Modern platform |

### Concerns

1. **Security**: Data protection, compliance
2. **Integration**: Will it connect with our ERP?
3. **Maintenance**: Who manages this long-term?
4. **Reliability**: Uptime, performance
5. **Resources**: IT effort during implementation

---

## Buying Behavior

### Decision Role

**Primary**: Technical Buyer / Gatekeeper
- Can't approve, but can block
- Validates technical fit
- Assesses security/risk
- May be brought in late (by champion)

### Evaluation Criteria

1. **Security & Compliance**: SOC 2, data residency, encryption
2. **Integration**: ERP connectors, API capability
3. **Architecture**: Cloud-native, scalable
4. **Support**: Vendor capabilities, SLAs
5. **IT Burden**: Implementation effort, ongoing maintenance

### Objections & Concerns

| Objection | Real Concern | How to Address |
|-----------|--------------|----------------|
| "Security concerns" | Data protection, compliance | SOC 2 cert, India data residency, encryption |
| "Integration is complex" | IT resource drain | Pre-built connectors, 2-4 week integration |
| "Who maintains this?" | Ongoing IT burden | Cloud-native, vendor-managed, minimal IT |
| "We have IT standards" | Technology governance | Technical documentation, architecture review |
| "What about uptime?" | Business continuity | 99.9% SLA, redundancy, DR |

---

## Engagement Strategy

### Content That Resonates

| Content Type | CIO Interest | Notes |
|--------------|-------------|-------|
| Security documentation | Very High | SOC 2, compliance |
| API documentation | High | Integration capability |
| Architecture overview | High | Technical credibility |
| Integration case studies | Medium | Proof of ERP connectivity |
| Technical deep-dive | Medium | For validation |
| ROI/business case | Low | Not their primary concern |

### Messaging That Resonates

**Do Say**:
- "SOC 2 certified, India data residency"
- "Pre-built SAP/Oracle connectors"
- "0.5 FTE IT involvement during implementation"
- "Cloud-native, we manage infrastructure"
- "99.9% uptime SLA"

**Don't Say**:
- "It's just business software"
- "IT doesn't need to be involved"
- "Integration is easy" (they've heard this)
- Technical claims without documentation

### Engagement Approach

- Engage proactively before technical review
- Provide documentation upfront
- Offer technical deep-dive session
- Answer security questionnaire proactively
- Make IT a partner, not a checkpoint

# BUYING COMMITTEE DYNAMICS

## Typical Buying Committee

| Role | Person | Priority | Engagement |
|------|--------|----------|------------|
| Champion | VP Logistics | Must have | Early, deep |
| Economic Buyer | CFO | Must have | Mid-late, business case |
| Technical Buyer | CIO | Should have | Mid, validation |
| User Buyer | Dispatch Manager | Should have | Demo, pilot |
| Influencer | Plant Head(s) | Nice to have | As needed |

## Engagement Sequence

```
Week 1-2: Champion Discovery
- Understand pain, validate fit
- Build relationship and trust
- Get internal landscape

Week 3-4: Stakeholder Mapping
- Identify buying committee
- Understand dynamics
- Plan engagement

Week 5-6: Champion + User Demo
- Show solution to champion
- Include user buyers
- Build internal momentum

Week 7-8: Technical Validation
- CIO/IT engagement
- Security/integration review
- Address technical concerns

Week 9-10: Business Case
- CFO engagement
- ROI presentation
- Reference connections

Week 11-12: Decision Push
- Full committee alignment
- Proposal presentation
- Negotiation
```
