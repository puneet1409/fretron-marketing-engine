# Fretron ICP Reference

Comprehensive Ideal Customer Profile for lead qualification and targeting.

## Primary ICP Definition

### Firmographic Profile

| Attribute | Ideal | Acceptable | Disqualifying |
|-----------|-------|------------|---------------|
| **Revenue** | ₹200-1000 Cr | ₹100-200 Cr or ₹1000-2000 Cr | <₹50 Cr or >₹5000 Cr |
| **Industry** | Manufacturing (Steel, Cement, Chemicals) | FMCG, Auto Parts, Pharma | Services, Retail (non-logistics focus) |
| **Shipments/Day** | 100-500 | 50-100 or 500-1000 | <20 or >2000 |
| **Geography** | India (multi-plant) | India (single plant) | International-only focus |
| **Plants/Warehouses** | 3-10 | 1-2 or 10-20 | 0 (asset-light) |

### Technographic Profile

| Attribute | Ideal | Acceptable | Disqualifying |
|-----------|-------|------------|---------------|
| **Current State** | Excel/Manual | Partial digitization | Locked into competitor TMS |
| **ERP** | SAP/Oracle (no TMS module) | Tally, regional ERP | Complex multi-ERP landscape |
| **IT Maturity** | Moderate (willing to adopt) | High (discerning) | Very low (resistant to tech) |
| **Cloud Comfort** | Comfortable | Learning | Cloud-averse |

### Behavioral Signals

| Signal | Ideal | Acceptable | Disqualifying |
|--------|-------|------------|---------------|
| **Trigger** | Active (new leader, expansion) | Planned (next 6 months) | None visible |
| **Engagement** | Multiple touchpoints | Some engagement | Competitor customer (happy) |
| **Budget** | Approved/available | Planned | No budget discussion possible |
| **Timeline** | Now - 3 months | 3-6 months | 12+ months |

## Industry Verticals (Prioritized)

### Tier 1: Primary Focus

#### Steel & Metals
**Why They Buy**:
- High shipment complexity (plant to port to customer)
- Weight-based logistics challenges
- Customer (OEM) penalty exposure
- High freight spend

**Typical Profile**:
- 200-500 shipments/day
- ₹50-200 Cr annual freight spend
- Multiple plants + export points
- OEM customer base with SLAs

**Key Personas**:
- VP/GM Logistics
- Plant Heads
- CFO (cost focus)

**Common Triggers**:
- OEM penalty notices
- New plant/capacity expansion
- Leadership change
- Cost reduction mandate

---

#### Cement
**Why They Buy**:
- Multi-plant coordination needs
- Dealer delivery SLA pressure
- Peak season capacity challenges
- Vehicle TAT issues

**Typical Profile**:
- 100-400 shipments/day per plant
- Wide distribution network (dealers)
- Seasonal demand patterns
- Fleet + hired vehicle mix

**Key Personas**:
- VP Supply Chain
- Regional Logistics Heads
- Distribution Heads

**Common Triggers**:
- New grinding unit
- Dealer complaints
- Peak season failures
- Fleet optimization project

---

#### Chemicals
**Why They Buy**:
- Compliance requirements (hazmat)
- Safety and documentation needs
- Customer-specific delivery requirements
- Complex routing

**Typical Profile**:
- 50-200 shipments/day
- Specialized carrier requirements
- Documentation-heavy
- Regulatory compliance pressure

**Key Personas**:
- VP Operations
- EHS Head (safety angle)
- Logistics Manager

**Common Triggers**:
- Compliance audit failure
- Safety incident
- Customer audit requirements
- Cost pressure

## Buying Committee Mapping

### Typical Roles

| Role | Title Variations | Buying Role | Key Concerns |
|------|-----------------|-------------|--------------|
| **Economic Buyer** | CFO, VP Finance | Approves budget | ROI, TCO, payback period |
| **Champion** | VP/Head Logistics, SCM Head | Drives evaluation | Visibility, efficiency, ease of use |
| **Technical Buyer** | CIO, IT Head | Validates fit | Integration, security, maintenance |
| **User Buyer** | Dispatch Manager, Ops Manager | Daily user | Usability, training, workload |
| **Influencer** | Plant Head, Regional Head | Stakeholder input | Local needs, implementation impact |

### Engagement Priority

```
1. CHAMPION (Must Have)
   - Primary contact
   - Internal advocate
   - Drives timeline

2. ECONOMIC BUYER (Must Have)
   - Budget authority
   - ROI focus
   - Final approval

3. USER BUYER (Should Have)
   - Implementation success
   - Adoption driver
   - Reference potential

4. TECHNICAL BUYER (Should Have)
   - Integration blocker
   - Security concerns
   - Maintenance worries

5. INFLUENCER (Nice to Have)
   - Political dynamics
   - Local requirements
   - Expansion potential
```

## Qualification Criteria

### BANT+T Framework

**B - Budget**
- Is there approved budget? → 3 points
- Is budget being planned? → 2 points
- No budget discussion? → 0 points

**A - Authority**
- Speaking to decision-maker? → 3 points
- Speaking to strong influencer? → 2 points
- Speaking to researcher/analyst? → 1 point

**N - Need**
- Urgent, quantified pain? → 3 points
- Acknowledged need? → 2 points
- Vague dissatisfaction? → 1 point

**T - Timeline**
- Decision in 0-3 months? → 3 points
- Decision in 3-6 months? → 2 points
- Decision in 6-12 months? → 1 point

**T - Trigger**
- Clear trigger event? → 3 points
- Planned change? → 2 points
- No trigger visible? → 1 point

### Score Interpretation

| Score | Qualification | Action |
|-------|---------------|--------|
| 13-15 | Sales-Ready | Immediate AE engagement |
| 10-12 | Warm | SDR development + AE intro |
| 7-9 | Cool | SDR nurture sequence |
| 4-6 | Cold | Marketing nurture only |
| <4 | Unqualified | Disqualify |

## Disqualification Criteria

### Hard Disqualifiers

- **Wrong Geography**: No India operations
- **Wrong Industry**: No logistics/shipping need
- **Competitor Lock-in**: Multi-year SAP TM / Oracle TMS
- **Too Small**: <20 shipments/day
- **Too Large**: >5000 Cr (enterprise sales motion needed)
- **Consultant/Researcher**: No buying authority
- **Student**: Academic inquiry

### Soft Disqualifiers (May Still Nurture)

- **Wrong Timing**: 12+ months out
- **No Budget**: But need is clear
- **Junior Contact**: But can introduce champion
- **Partial Fit**: Some but not all ICP criteria

## Quick Qualification Script

```
"Thanks for your interest. To make sure I connect you with
the right person, can I ask a few quick questions?

1. VOLUME: Approximately how many shipments per day?
   [<20 = DQ, 20-50 = cool, 50-500 = warm, >500 = check fit]

2. CURRENT STATE: How do you manage logistics tracking today?
   [Excel = ideal, partial system = good, full TMS = probe more]

3. TRIGGER: What's driving your interest in this now?
   [Specific event = hot, planning = warm, exploring = cool]

4. TIMELINE: When are you looking to make a decision?
   [Now-3mo = hot, 3-6mo = warm, 6-12mo = nurture]

5. ROLE: What's your role in this evaluation?
   [Decision-maker = ideal, influencer = good, researcher = nurture]

Based on what you've shared, [appropriate next step]."
```
