# Account Selection Framework for Fretron

Systematic approach to identifying and prioritizing high-value target accounts.

## Account Tiering Model

### Tier Structure

| Tier | Definition | Annual Value | Account Count | Coverage Model |
|------|------------|--------------|---------------|----------------|
| Tier 1 | Strategic | ₹50L+ ARR potential | 20-30 accounts | Named AE + SDR |
| Tier 2 | Growth | ₹20-50L ARR potential | 50-100 accounts | Named AE, shared SDR |
| Tier 3 | Standard | ₹5-20L ARR potential | 200-500 accounts | Pooled coverage |
| Tier 4 | Programmatic | <₹5L ARR potential | 1000+ accounts | Marketing-led |

### Tier Allocation Guidelines

**Tier 1 (20-30 accounts)**:
- Handpicked by sales leadership
- Executive-level engagement planned
- Custom content and campaigns
- Quarterly business reviews built-in

**Tier 2 (50-100 accounts)**:
- Selected through scoring model
- Industry cluster approach
- Personalized outreach
- Monthly review cadence

**Tier 3 (200-500 accounts)**:
- Scored and filtered from TAM
- Industry/segment plays
- Semi-personalized outreach
- Campaign-based engagement

**Tier 4 (Programmatic)**:
- All ICP-fit accounts not in T1-T3
- Automated nurture
- Inbound capture focus
- Promoted to T3 on engagement

## Account Research Protocol

### Tier 1 Account Research (Deep)

**Time Investment**: 2-3 hours per account

**Research Areas**:

1. **Company Overview**
   - Business model and revenue streams
   - Recent performance (growth, challenges)
   - Strategic priorities (annual report, investor calls)
   - Organizational structure

2. **Logistics Profile**
   - Shipment volume estimates
   - Distribution network footprint
   - Key logistics challenges (news, job postings)
   - Current technology stack

3. **Buying Committee**
   - Key stakeholders identified
   - Reporting structure mapped
   - LinkedIn profiles reviewed
   - Mutual connections identified

4. **Trigger Analysis**
   - Recent news and announcements
   - Leadership changes
   - Expansion plans
   - Pain indicators

5. **Competitive Intel**
   - Current vendors (if known)
   - Past evaluations
   - Peer company decisions

**Output**: Account brief (1-2 pages) + personalization points

---

### Tier 2 Account Research (Standard)

**Time Investment**: 30-60 minutes per account

**Research Areas**:

1. **Company Snapshot**
   - Size, industry, locations
   - Basic business model
   - Recent news (last 6 months)

2. **Logistics Indicators**
   - Volume estimates
   - Key challenges (inferred)
   - Technology gaps

3. **Key Contacts**
   - 2-3 target personas identified
   - Basic LinkedIn review

4. **Entry Point**
   - Best angle for outreach
   - Relevant content/case study

**Output**: Account card (half page) + outreach angle

---

### Tier 3 Account Research (Light)

**Time Investment**: 10-15 minutes per account

**Research Areas**:

1. **Fit Validation**
   - Confirm ICP criteria
   - No disqualifying factors

2. **Personalization Hook**
   - One specific detail for outreach
   - Industry relevance

3. **Contact Identification**
   - Primary target persona

**Output**: Personalization note + contact info

## Account List Building Process

### Step 1: Define Total Addressable Market (TAM)

**Fretron TAM Criteria**:
```
Industry: Manufacturing (Steel, Cement, Chemicals, FMCG, Auto, Pharma)
Revenue: ₹50 Cr - ₹5000 Cr
Geography: India (primary operations)
Shipments: 20+ per day estimated
```

**Sources**:
- Industry databases (Dun & Bradstreet, ZoomInfo India)
- Industry association member lists
- Chamber of Commerce directories
- LinkedIn Sales Navigator searches

---

### Step 2: Apply ICP Filters

**Filter Sequence**:
```
1. Industry filter → Reduces TAM by ~70%
2. Revenue filter → Reduces by ~50%
3. Location filter → Reduces by ~30%
4. Exclusion filter → Removes competitors, non-fits
```

**Exclusion Criteria**:
- Known SAP TM / Oracle OTM customers (locked in)
- Companies with <20 shipments/day
- Pure services companies (no physical logistics)
- Companies in active legal/financial distress

---

### Step 3: Score and Rank

**Scoring Process**:
```
1. Apply fit scoring (automated where possible)
2. Enrich with intent data (if available)
3. Add relationship scoring (manual input)
4. Calculate total score
5. Rank order
```

**Tools**:
- Spreadsheet for initial scoring
- CRM for ongoing tracking
- Intent data platform (if budget allows)

---

### Step 4: Allocate to Tiers

**Allocation Process**:
```
1. Top 20-30 by score → Tier 1 (review with sales leadership)
2. Next 50-100 → Tier 2 (validate with AEs)
3. Next 200-500 → Tier 3 (automated assignment)
4. Remainder ICP-fit → Tier 4 (marketing nurture)
```

**Quarterly Review**:
- Promote high-engagement Tier 3/4 to higher tiers
- Demote non-engaging Tier 1/2 accounts
- Add new accounts from market monitoring

## Account Monitoring System

### Signal Tracking

**Weekly Monitoring (Tier 1)**:
- Google Alerts on company name
- LinkedIn activity of key contacts
- Job postings (logistics/SCM roles)
- News mentions

**Monthly Monitoring (Tier 2)**:
- Industry news scan
- LinkedIn updates on key contacts
- Quarterly results (if public)

**Quarterly Monitoring (Tier 3)**:
- Batch review of firmographic changes
- Intent signal aggregation
- Engagement review for tier promotion

### Trigger Response Playbook

| Trigger | Response Time | Action |
|---------|---------------|--------|
| Leadership change | 48 hours | Personalized outreach |
| Expansion news | 72 hours | Relevant content share |
| Funding announcement | 1 week | Case study share |
| Competitor mention | 24 hours | Competitive positioning |
| Job posting (logistics) | 1 week | Pain-point outreach |
| Website visit (high intent) | 24 hours | Direct outreach |
| Content download | 48 hours | Nurture sequence |
