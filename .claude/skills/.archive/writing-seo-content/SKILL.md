# Writing SEO Content for Fretron

Create high-quality articles that rank in search and drive qualified organic traffic.

## When to Use This Skill

- Writing blog posts for organic traffic
- Creating SEO landing pages
- Optimizing existing content for rankings
- Building pillar content and topic clusters
- Writing comparison or "best of" articles

## SEO Content Framework

### Step 1: Understand Search Intent
Before writing, know what the searcher wants.

| Intent Type | Searcher Wants | Content Format |
|-------------|----------------|----------------|
| Informational | Learn something | How-to guide, explainer |
| Commercial | Compare options | Comparison, review, list |
| Transactional | Take action | Landing page, pricing |
| Navigational | Find specific page | Product/brand page |

**Check intent**: Google the keyword, see what ranks. That's what Google thinks searchers want.

### Step 2: Research Competing Content
Before writing, analyze top 5-10 ranking pages:

- What topics do they cover?
- How long are they?
- What format (list, guide, how-to)?
- What's missing or weak?
- What angle could be different?

### Step 3: Create Content Brief

```markdown
## Content Brief: [Title]

**Target Keyword**: [primary keyword]
**Secondary Keywords**: [list 3-5]
**Search Intent**: [informational/commercial/transactional]
**Word Count Target**: [based on competitors]
**Content Format**: [guide/list/comparison/how-to]

**Key Topics to Cover**:
1. [topic from competitor analysis]
2. [topic from competitor analysis]
3. [unique angle or missing topic]

**Unique Value**:
[What makes this better than existing content?]

**Target Audience**:
[Specific reader persona]

**CTA/Conversion Goal**:
[What action should reader take?]
```

## On-Page SEO Checklist

### Title Tag (Most Important)
- Include primary keyword
- Front-load keyword when natural
- Keep under 60 characters
- Make it compelling (not just keywords)

**Good**: "How to Reduce Freight Costs: 7 Proven Strategies for Indian Shippers"
**Bad**: "Freight Cost Reduction Tips and Strategies for Logistics Optimization"

### Meta Description
- Include primary keyword
- 150-160 characters
- Include value proposition or hook
- End with soft CTA when appropriate

### URL Structure
- Short and descriptive
- Include primary keyword
- Use hyphens, not underscores
- Avoid dates in URL (evergreen)

**Good**: `/blog/reduce-freight-costs`
**Bad**: `/blog/2024/01/15/how-to-reduce-freight-costs-in-logistics`

### Headings (H1, H2, H3)
- One H1 only (usually = title)
- H2s for main sections (include keyword variations)
- H3s for subsections
- Make them scannable and descriptive

### Content Body
- Include primary keyword in first 100 words
- Use keyword variations naturally throughout
- Include related terms (semantic SEO)
- Answer the search intent clearly
- Use short paragraphs (2-3 sentences)

### Images
- Descriptive file names (freight-cost-chart.png)
- Alt text with keyword when relevant
- Compress for page speed

### Internal Links
- Link to related content on your site
- Use descriptive anchor text
- Link to high-priority pages

### External Links
- Link to authoritative sources
- Adds credibility (E-E-A-T)
- Opens in new tab

## Content Structure Templates

```

[2-3 sentence intro explaining what reader will learn]

[Brief definition/context]

## Why [Topic] Matters

## How to [Achieve Outcome]: Step-by-Step

### Step 1: [Action]
[Explanation + example]

[Explanation + example]

### Step 3: [Action]
[Explanation + example]

[Summary tips]

[Recap + CTA]

[Schema-friendly Q&A]
```

## E-E-A-T Signals (Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trust)

Google values content from credible sources. Build E-E-A-T:

### Experience
- Include real examples and case studies
- Share specific numbers from actual implementations
- Reference customer outcomes

### Expertise
- Author bylines with credentials
- Cite specific domain knowledge
- Cover topics thoroughly

### Authoritativeness
- Get backlinks from industry sites
- Be cited by others
- Consistent topic coverage

### Trust
- Clear about who you are
- Contact information
- Accurate, fact-checked content
- HTTPS, good UX

## Content Quality Checklist

Before publishing:

- [ ] Answers search intent clearly
- [ ] Better than top 3 competitors
- [ ] Primary keyword in title, H1, first 100 words
- [ ] Proper heading hierarchy (H1→H2→H3)
- [ ] Internal links to related content
- [ ] External links to authoritative sources
- [ ] Images with alt text
- [ ] Meta title under 60 chars
- [ ] Meta description 150-160 chars
- [ ] Clear CTA / next step
- [ ] Proofread and fact-checked
- [ ] Mobile-friendly formatting

## Content Performance Tracking

Monitor after publishing:
- Rankings for target keyword (weekly)
- Organic traffic to page (monthly)
- Time on page / bounce rate
- Conversions from page

**Update content** if:
- Rankings drop
- Competitors publish better content
- Information becomes outdated
- Conversion rate is low
