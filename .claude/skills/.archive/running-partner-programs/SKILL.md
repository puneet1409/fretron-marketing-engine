# Running Partner Programs for Fretron

Build ecosystem partnerships that amplify reach and borrow credibility.

## When to Use This Skill

- Identifying potential partner types for Fretron
- Designing partner program structure and incentives
- Planning co-marketing campaigns with partners
- Creating partner enablement materials
- Measuring partner-sourced pipeline

## Trust Philosophy Integration

**Partners accelerate trust-building.**

Partners provide:
- **Borrowed Credibility**: Their endorsement transfers trust
- **Warm Introductions**: Skip Trust Level 0-2
- **Contextual Relevance**: Partner understands customer's world

**Principle**: Choose partners whose trust with your ICP is already established.

## Partner Program Design

### "Give to Get" Framework

**Principle**: Create mutual value exchange, not one-sided referrals.

```
FRETRON GIVES                    PARTNER GETS
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Revenue share on referrals   →   New revenue stream
Co-marketing budget          →   Amplified reach
Joint content creation       →   Thought leadership
Technical integration        →   Enhanced offering
Customer success support     →   Client satisfaction
```

### Program Tiers

**Partner Level: Associate**
- Requirements: Sign agreement, complete training
- Benefits: 10% referral commission, partner portal access
- Co-marketing: Listed on partner page

**Partner Level: Certified**
- Requirements: 3+ referrals, certification exam
- Benefits: 15% commission, co-marketing funds (₹1L/year)
- Co-marketing: Joint case study, webinar participation

**Partner Level: Premier**
- Requirements: 10+ referrals, dedicated resource
- Benefits: 20% commission, co-marketing funds (₹5L/year)
- Co-marketing: Joint events, executive alignment, roadmap input

### Referral Tracking

**Referral Registration**:
1. Partner submits lead via portal
2. Fretron qualifies within 48 hours
3. Lead assigned, partner notified
4. Progress updates at each stage
5. Commission paid on first invoice

**Commission Structure**:
- New customer referral: 10-20% of Year 1 contract
- Expansion referral: 10% of incremental value
- Renewal influence: 5% of renewal value

## Co-Marketing Playbooks

### Playbook 1: Joint Webinar

**Structure**:
- 60-minute format
- 15 min: Industry context (partner presents)
- 20 min: Solution overview (Fretron presents)
- 15 min: Case study (joint or customer)
- 10 min: Q&A

**Promotion**:
- Partner promotes to their audience
- Fretron promotes to target accounts
- Shared landing page, lead split

**Follow-up**:
- Leads distributed by source
- Joint nurture for engaged attendees
- Sales follow-up on hot leads

### Playbook 2: Joint Content

**Content Types**:
- Industry report (co-branded)
- Best practices guide
- Assessment tool
- Case study featuring mutual customer

**Process**:
1. Agree on topic and audience
2. Fretron drafts, partner reviews
3. Co-brand with both logos
4. Promote through both channels
5. Gate behind shared form

### Playbook 3: Event Collaboration

**Options**:
- Co-sponsor industry event booth
- Joint executive dinner (10-15 attendees)
- Partner conference session
- Customer roundtable

**Budget Sharing**:
- Booth: 50/50 or proportional to company size
- Dinner: Host pays, partner provides attendees
- Content: Fretron creates, partner distributes

### Playbook 4: Integration Marketing

**For Technology Partners**:
- Joint press release on integration
- Technical documentation co-created
- Integration highlighted in both product tours
- Customer success stories featuring integration

## Partner Identification Process

### Step 1: Map the Ecosystem

List all companies that:
- Serve your ICP (manufacturing, logistics)
- Solve adjacent problems (ERP, fleet, warehouse)
- Have trusted relationships with decision-makers

### Step 2: Score Potential Partners

| Criteria | Weight | Scoring |
|----------|--------|---------|
| ICP overlap | 30% | High/Medium/Low |
| Relationship quality | 25% | None/Some/Strong |
| Strategic fit | 20% | Competitive/Neutral/Complementary |
| Partner program maturity | 15% | None/Basic/Developed |
| Resource capacity | 10% | Low/Medium/High |

### Step 3: Prioritize and Approach

**Warm Approach** (if relationship exists):
- Executive-to-executive introduction
- Reference mutual customers
- Propose specific collaboration

**Cold Approach** (if no relationship):
- Partner program website inquiry
- LinkedIn connection to partner manager
- Conference networking

## Common Pitfalls

1. **One-Sided Programs**: All take, no give = partner churn
2. **Too Many Partners**: Dilutes focus, reduces enablement quality
3. **Poor Lead Routing**: Slow response = partner frustration
4. **No Enablement**: Partners can't sell what they don't understand
5. **Commission Disputes**: Unclear attribution kills trust
