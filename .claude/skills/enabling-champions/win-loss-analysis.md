# Win/Loss Analysis Framework

Systematic approach to understanding why deals are won or lost and applying insights.

---

## Win/Loss Philosophy

### Why Win/Loss Matters

- **Win Rate**: Understanding wins helps replicate success
- **Loss Prevention**: Understanding losses helps avoid future ones
- **Competitive Intel**: Direct insight into how competitors sell
- **Product Feedback**: Real market feedback on capabilities
- **Sales Enablement**: What messaging and tactics work?
- **Marketing Insight**: Which leads convert? Why?

### Analysis Principles

1. **Interview, Don't Survey**: Rich insights come from conversation
2. **Third-Party When Possible**: Buyers are more honest with neutral parties
3. **Timely**: Conduct within 30 days of decision
4. **Systematic**: Every significant deal, not just convenient ones
5. **Actionable**: Insights must drive changes

---

## Deal Classification

### Win Types

| Type | Definition | Analysis Priority |
|------|------------|-------------------|
| Competitive Win | Chose Fretron over named competitor | High |
| Status Quo Win | Chose Fretron over doing nothing | Medium |
| Incumbent Displacement | Replaced existing solution | High |
| Greenfield Win | First TMS implementation | Medium |

### Loss Types

| Type | Definition | Analysis Priority |
|------|------------|-------------------|
| Competitive Loss | Lost to named competitor | High |
| No Decision | Prospect chose to do nothing | High |
| Incumbent Stay | Stayed with current solution | Medium |
| Budget Kill | Project cancelled/defunded | Low |
| Bad Fit | We disqualified or they did | Low |

### Analysis Thresholds

**Must Analyze**:
- All competitive wins/losses >â‚¹5L ARR
- All deals involving strategic accounts
- All deals with new product/feature requests

**Should Analyze**:
- All competitive wins/losses
- All no-decision losses
- Deals with unusual sales cycles

**Optional**:
- Small deals with clear reasons
- Bad fit disqualifications

## Interview Process

### Interview Timing

| Outcome | Interview Window | Follow-Up |
|---------|------------------|-----------|
| Win | 2-4 weeks post-close | 90-day check-in |
| Loss | 1-3 weeks post-decision | None (don't pester) |
| No Decision | 2-4 weeks after go-dark | Quarterly check |

### Who Conducts Interviews?

**Internal Options**:
- Customer Success (for wins)
- Sales Leadership (selective)
- Product Marketing

**External Options (Preferred for Losses)**:
- Third-party research firm
- Consultant or advisor
- Industry analyst

## Interview Guides

### Win Interview Questions (30 min)

**Opening (2 min)**:
```
"Thanks for taking the time. I want to understand your buying
journey so we can help future companies like yours. This is
completely about learningâ€”not selling you anything else.

Mind if I ask some questions about your decision process?"
```

**The Journey (10 min)**:
```
TRIGGER:
"Take me back to the beginning. What triggered the search
for a TMS solution?"

"Was there a specific event or moment that made this a priority?"

EVALUATION:
"How did you first hear about Fretron?"

"Who else did you consider? How did you narrow it down?"

"What were your top 2-3 criteria when evaluating options?"
```

**The Decision (10 min)**:
```
DIFFERENTIATORS:
"What ultimately made you choose Fretron over [alternatives]?"

"Was there a specific moment or factor that tipped the decision?"

"What did we do well during the sales process?"

CONCERNS:
"What almost made you NOT choose us?"

"Were there any concerns you had going in? Did we address them?"

"What could we have done better in the process?"
```

**Competitive Intel (5 min)**:
```
"Without naming names if you prefer, how did [competitor]
compare in your evaluation?"

"What did they do well?"

"Where did they fall short?"
```

**Closing (3 min)**:
```
"If you were advising a peer considering a TMS, what would
you tell them about the process?"

"Anything else I should know?"

"Thanks so much. Would you be open to being a reference for
similar companies in the future?"
```

### Loss Interview Questions (20 min)

**Opening (2 min)**:
```
"Thanks for taking the time. I know you went a different
direction, and that's totally fine. I'm here to learn so
we can improveâ€”this isn't about changing your mind.

Would you be comfortable sharing some honest feedback?"
```

**Understanding the Decision (10 min)**:
```
OUTCOME:
"Can you tell me what you ultimately decided to do?"

"What drove that decision?"

PROCESS:
"Looking back at your evaluation, what were your top criteria?"

"How did Fretron stack up on those criteria?"

THE GAP:
"Where did we fall short compared to [chosen solution]?"

"Was there anything we didn't understand about your needs?"

"Was there a specific moment where you felt Fretron wasn't
the right fit?"
```

**What Could Have Changed (5 min)**:
```
"Looking back, is there anything we could have done differently
that might have changed the outcome?"

"Was there information we didn't provide that would have helped?"

"How was your experience with our sales team? The demo?"
```

**Competitive Intel (3 min)**:
```
"What did [winner] do particularly well?"

"If you were advising them on how to improve, what would you say?"
```

### Loss Debrief Session

**Format**: 30-minute team discussion
**Attendees**: AE, SDR, Sales Manager, Product Marketing

**Agenda**:
1. AE shares loss summary (5 min)
2. Review interview findings (10 min)
3. Identify what we could control (5 min)
4. Determine action items (10 min)

**Ground Rules**:
- No blame, only learning
- Focus on systemic improvements
- Assign specific follow-ups
