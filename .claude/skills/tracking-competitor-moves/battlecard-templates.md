## Objection Handling

**"[Competitor] has better brand recognition"**
> "True, [Competitor] is well-known. The question is: what matters more - brand or results? Our customers chose us because [specific differentiator]. Here's what [reference customer] achieved..."

**"[Competitor] is cheaper"**
> "Let's look at total cost of ownership. Their license model means [hidden costs]. Our transaction pricing means [benefit]. Over 3 years, customers typically find..."

**"[Competitor] has more features"**
> "More features isn't always better if you don't need them. What matters is: do they solve YOUR specific challenges? Let me show you how we handle [their specific need]..."

**"We're already in talks with [Competitor]"**
> "That's great - you're doing your due diligence. Before you decide, I'd just ask: have them show you [specific weakness area]. And let me show you how we handle [our strength area]. Then you'll have a complete picture."

---

## Landmine Questions

Questions to ask prospects that expose competitor weaknesses:

1. **"Ask them about implementation timeline and resources required."**
   - They'll likely say 6-12 months, significant IT involvement

2. **"Ask for India-based customer references in your industry."**
   - They may struggle to provide relevant local references

3. **"Ask about their pricing model for volume fluctuations."**
   - License models hurt when volume drops

4. **"Ask about their local support and training."**
   - May be US/Europe-centric support

---

## Discovery Questions

Ask these early to understand competitive situation:

1. "Are you evaluating other solutions? Which ones?"
2. "What's your impression of [Competitor] so far?"
3. "What matters most in your decision - [our strength] or [their strength]?"
4. "Have you looked at their India implementation track record?"
